Amazon Rain Forest

Fun Fact

The Amazon Rainforest is the world's largest rainforest, spanning over 6.7 million square kilometers and holding approximately 10% of the world's known species

Taiga Forest

Fun Fact

The Taiga, also known as the boreal forest, is the world's largest land biome, covering parts of North America, Europe, and Asia. It's a cold, coniferous forest with long, harsh winters and short summers

Congo Forest

Fun Fact

The Congo is home to the second largest French-speaking city in the world, Kinshasa, and boasts one of the world's most biodiverse regions, the Congo Basin, which is larger than Alaska.

Valdivian Forest

Fun Fact

A fun fact about Valdivia, a city in Chile, is that it's known for its beautiful riverside location and the presence of the famous black-necked swan.

Tongass Forest

Fun Fact

The Tongass National Forest, located in Southeast Alaska, is a fun fact because it's the world's largest remaining temperate rainforest

Sundarbans Forest

Fun Fact

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the world's largest coastal mangrove forest, home to the Royal Bengal tiger.

Borneo Forest

Fun Fact

Borneo is the third-largest island in the world, not a country, and home to the world's oldest rainforest.

Daintree Forest

Fun Fact

The Daintree Rainforest in Queensland, Australia, is a fascinating natural wonder with numerous unique features.
