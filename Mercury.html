Antarctic Desert

Fun Fact

Even though it's covered in ice, it's technically a desert because it gets less than 50 mm (2 inches) of precipitation per year—drier than the Sahara!

Arctic Desert

Fun Fact

The Arctic Desert is home to polar bears, but no penguins—those are only in the Southern Hemisphere!

Sahara Desert

Fun Fact

The Sahara is so big, it’s roughly the size of the United States! Some parts haven’t seen rain in decades.

Arabian Desert

Fun Fact

The Rub' al Khali (Empty Quarter) within it has dunes as high as skyscrapers—up to 250 meters (820 feet)!

Gobi Desert

Fun Fact

It can get below -40°C in winter, and was part of the route used by Genghis Khan during his empire’s expansion.

Kalahari Desert

Fun Fact

Despite being called a desert, the Kalahari supports lions, elephants, and meerkats—and even gets rain!

Patagonian Desert

Fun Fact

Wind here is so strong it can sculpt rocks, and the desert is home to fossils of dinosaurs from the Jurassic era!

Great Victoria Desert

Fun Fact

Despite its harsh conditions, it has over 400 plant species and is home to many Indigenous Australian communities.
