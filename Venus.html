Mauna Loa

Fun Fact

Mauna Loa is the largest volcano on Earth by volume and taller than Mount Everest if measured from its underwater base!

Mount Vesuvius

Fun Fact

In 79 AD, Vesuvius buried Pompeii and Herculaneum under ash — preserving them for archaeologists nearly 2,000 years later!

Mount Fuji

Fun Fact

Mount Fuji is not just a volcano — it’s a sacred symbol of Japan and a popular subject in art and photography.

Eyjafjallajökull

Fun Fact

Its 2010 eruption grounded flights across Europe for weeks — and made headlines for its tongue-twister name!

Mount Kilimanjaro

Fun Fact

It’s Africa’s tallest mountain, and though dormant, it’s the world’s tallest free-standing volcano — with glaciers on top!

Krakatoa

Fun Fact

Its 1883 eruption was one of the loudest sounds ever recorded — heard 3,000 miles away!

Mount Etna

Fun Fact

Mount Etna is Europe’s most active volcano, and ancient Romans believed it was the forge of Vulcan, god of fire.

Mount St. Helens

Fun Fact

Its 1980 eruption was so powerful it blew off the top of the mountain and reduced its height by 400 meters!
