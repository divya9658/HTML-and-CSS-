Greenland

Fun Fact

The largest island in the world, Greenland is primarily covered in ice and glaciers. It's an autonomous territory of Denmark, but it has a primarily Inuit population.

New Guinea Island

Fun Fact

The second-largest island, New Guinea is known for its extremely wet climate and relatively sparse population.

Borneo Island

Fun Fact

Borneo is the third-largest island and the only island shared by three countries: Malaysia, Indonesia, and Brunei. It's known for its dense rainforests and incredibly diverse flora and fauna.

Madagascar Island

Fun Fact

The fourth-largest island, Madagascar is known for its unique wildlife, including numerous lemurs. It's also the largest island in the Indian Ocean.

Baffin Island

Fun Fact

Located in Canada, Baffin Island is the largest island within the country and is known for its rugged, remote terrain, including glaciers, ice caps, and fjords.

Sumatra Island

Fun Fact

Part of the Indonesian archipelago, Sumatra is known for its lush rainforests and is home to the Sumatran elephant, orangutan, rhinoceros, and tiger.

Honshu Island

Fun Fact

The main island of Japan, Honshu is a bustling hub with a rich culture and history.

Victoria Island

Fun Fact

Located in Canada, Victoria Island is known for its remote location and vast, relatively unexplored landscapes.
