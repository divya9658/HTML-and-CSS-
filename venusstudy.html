Uncover Earth’s fiery mountains by studying the volcanic terrain of Venus.

What is a Volcano?

A volcano is a rupture in the planet's crust, allowing magma, ash, and gases to escape. They create mountains and islands, shape landscapes, and support unique ecosystems.

Scientific Deep Dive

  • Geology:Lava flow, calderas, plate tectonics, hotspot activity.
  • Ecology: Volcanic soil supports rich agriculture. Life returns quickly.
  • Climate: Eruptions can alter climate by sending ash into the atmosphere.

Earth & Space Comparison

enus has over 1,600 major volcanoes — more than any planet in the solar system. Though its surface is extremely hot (up to 475°C), radar images suggest past and possibly present volcanic activity

Landmark Studies

🔥 Mauna Loa – Largest volcano on Earth, located in Hawaii. Shield volcano with gentle slopes.

⚙️ Mount Vesuvius – Italy’s infamous volcano. Destroyed Pompeii in 79 AD.

🏔 Mount Fuji – Iconic stratovolcano in Japan. Cultural symbol and pilgrimage site.

❄ Eyjafjallajökull – Icelandic volcano famous for 2010 ash cloud that disrupted air travel.

🏔 Mount Kilimanjaro – Dormant stratovolcano in Tanzania. Africa’s highest peak with glaciers.

⚡ Krakatoa – Volcanic island in Indonesia. Known for the 1883 cataclysmic eruption.

🔥 Mount Etna – Europe’s most active volcano in Sicily, Italy. Frequently erupts lava and ash.

⛈ Mount St. Helens – Located in Washington, USA. Notable for its explosive 1980 eruption.
