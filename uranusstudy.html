What is a Glacier?

A glacier is a large, slow-moving body of ice that forms on land and is a key part of the Earth's cryosphere.

Scientific Deep Dive

  • Formation: Snow accumulates, compacts, and transforms into firn, which eventually turns into glacial ice.
  • Glacial Movemen: Glaciers flow under the influence of gravity, and the rate of movement varies based on temperature, slope, and ice thickness.
  • Glacial Erosion: Glaciers carve valleys, create U-shaped valleys, and deposit sediments as they move.

Earth & Space Comparison

While glaciers don't exist on other planets, the concept of ice caps and polar ice sheets is relevant.
