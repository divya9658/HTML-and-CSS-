Mountains of Mars---Peaks Beyond Earth.

What is a mountain?

mountain is a large landform that rises prominently above its surroundings, typically having steep slopes, a summit (peak), and a significant elevation compared to nearby terrain.

Scientific Deep Dive

Earth & Space Comparison

Mars is located one planet farther from the Sun than Earth, making it colder and drier.

Landmark Studies

⛰ Mount Everest – The tallest mountain in the world, standing at 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet)

🗻 Mount Fuji – An iconic volcanic mountain in Japan, standing 3,776 meters (12,389 feet) tall. It’s a cultural symbol and popular for hiking and photography.

🏔 Mount Kilimanjaro – Africa’s highest peak at 5,895 meters (19,341 feet). This free-standing volcanic mountain is located in Tanzania and known for its snow-capped summit near the equator.

🌋 Mount K2 – The fifth highest peak at 8,485 meters (27,838 feet), located in the Himalayas on the Nepal-Tibet border. Known for its sharp pyramid shape.

🪘 Mount Makalu – The fifth highest peak at 8,485 meters (27,838 feet), located in the Himalayas on the Nepal-Tibet border. Known for its sharp pyramid shape.

🏖 Mount Cho Oyu – The sixth highest mountain in the world, 8,188 meters (26,864 feet) tall, located in the Himalayas near Everest. It’s considered one of the easier 8,000-meter peaks to climb.

🌬 Mount Dhaulagiri – Seventh highest in the world at 8,167 meters (26,795 feet), located in Nepal. It’s known for its massive, snowy peak and dangerous climbing routes.

🧗 Mount Manaslu – Eighth highest mountain at 8,163 meters (26,781 feet), also in Nepal. Its name means “Mountain of the Spirit,” and it’s popular among experienced climbers.
