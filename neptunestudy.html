What is a Islands?

An island is a landmass smaller than a continent that is entirely surrounded by water.

Scientific Deep Dive

  • Geology: Geological studies on islands can provide insights into plate tectonics, volcanic processes, and the history of the Earth's crust.
  • Glacial Movemen: Islands often have endemic species, which are found nowhere else on Earth, due to isolation and unique evolutionary pressures.
  • Glacial Erosion: Climate change research on islands helps understand the impacts of climate change on coastal communities and ecosystems.

Earth & Space Comparison

Islands are analogous to "microcosms" of Earth's ecosystems, providing a concentrated view of evolutionary processes and the impact of isolation.
