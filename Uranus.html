Seller Glacier

Fun Fact

The Seller Glacier, located on the western edge of the Antarctic Peninsula, is the largest individual glacier in the world, spanning over 7,000 square kilometers. It's also a significant source of tabular icebergs.

Thurston Glacier

Fun Fact

Thurston Island Glacier No. 1 is a massive glacier in Antarctica, ranking as the second largest after Seller Glacier. It's located on Thurston Island and has a surface area of 5,261 square kilometers (2,031 square miles)

Alexander Island Glacier No. 1 - Antarctica

Fun Fact

It's also one of the largest glaciers to have experienced significant retreat over the last two centuries, with scientists unsure whether this is a natural cycle or a result of climate change.

Alexander Island Glacier No.2

Fun Fact

Glaciers like this contribute to the overall fresh water storage in Antarctica, which accounts for a significant portion of the planet's fresh water reserves.Alexander Island itself is considered a large glacier complex, with multiple individual glaciers.

Baring Glacier

Fun Fact

it has surged at least six times in the 20th century, states the Michigan Tech Research Institute, most recently in 1993-1995. It's also known as the largest temperate surging glacier on Earth.

Malaspina-Seward glacier

Fun Fact

Malaspina is susceptible to climate change, having lost 20 meters of thickness in the last 50 years.

Lambert Glacier

Fun Fact
